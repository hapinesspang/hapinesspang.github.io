Home Measurement Toolkit respects user privacy. This policy explains what information the app uses and how measurement features and advertising work.

This app is an everyday reference measurement tool and does not guarantee professional equipment-level accuracy.

1. App Features

Home Measurement Toolkit uses iPhone sensors, the screen, and microphone permission to provide a level, angle meter, ruler, compass, sound meter, and saved measurement history.

2. Information Stored Directly by the App

The app can store measurement records saved by the user on the device.

  • Measurement tool type
  • Measurement value
  • Date and time saved

This information is stored on the user's device. The app does not operate a developer server and does not transmit saved measurement records to a developer-operated server.

3. Microphone Use

The sound meter requests microphone access to show a live reference sound level.

  • The microphone is used only for the sound meter feature.
  • The app does not save audio recordings.
  • The app does not transmit microphone audio to a developer-operated server.
  • Displayed dB values are reference values and may vary by device and environment.

4. Location Information

The current version of the app does not request location permission. The compass shows reference direction based on magnetic north.

5. Advertising

The app may use Google AdMob ads to support free availability. The app does not offer in-app purchases or subscriptions. During ad serving, the Google Mobile Ads SDK and related Google services may process certain information for ad delivery, ad measurement, fraud prevention, ad quality, diagnostics, and performance.

Examples of information that may be processed include:

  • Approximate location inferred from IP address
  • Device advertising identifier (IDFA), if allowed by the user
  • Ad impressions and ad interaction information
  • Device and app information
  • Diagnostics and performance information

6. Personalized Ads and Tracking Permission

The app may request Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT) permission for personalized ads and ad performance measurement. If the user allows tracking, the device advertising identifier (IDFA) may be used for personalized ads and ad measurement.

If the user does not allow tracking, all measurement tools, saved records, and ad privacy settings remain available. Ads may be non-personalized or limited.

Ad consent and tracking permission can be managed in the app where available or in iOS Settings.

7. Third-Party Services

The app may use the following third-party services:

  • Google AdMob
  • Google Mobile Ads SDK
  • Google User Messaging Platform (UMP) SDK

For more information about Google's privacy practices, see the Google Privacy Policy and Google AdMob.

8. Retention and Deletion

Measurement records saved by the user are stored on the user's device. Users can delete saved records inside the app.

The app does not provide account creation or login, so there is no developer account data to delete.

When the app is deleted, app data stored on the device is deleted according to iOS behavior.

Advertising-related information processed by Google is managed according to Google's policies and the user's consent status.

9. Children

This app is not directed to children and is not operated as a service for children under 13.

10. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated if app features, advertising practices, or legal requirements change. Updates will be posted in the app or on this distribution page.

11. Contact
